Research

My research uses historical maps, archival sources, spatial databases, and GIS-based analysis to study long-term spatial change, cultural landscapes, water systems, settlements, and human–environment relations in China and Inner Asia.

I am currently working on three areas. First, the relationship between Tibetan Buddhism and environmental change in historical periods, including the spatial dynamics of monasteries across the Qinghai–Tibet Plateau. Second, the historical geography of settlements in China, examining how communities organized themselves in relation to water, terrain, and political boundaries. Third, the reconstruction of hydrographic networks in arid Northwest China, tracing how irrigation and climate variability have reshaped surface water systems over the past century.

I am committed to making historical geographic information open, reproducible, and interoperable through digital humanities methods.
